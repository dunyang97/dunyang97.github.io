Research Experiences

Bionic Sea Urchin Robot with Self Adaptability to Complex Terrain

Beihang University (Department of Dynamics, Systems & Controls)
Mar 2021 – Present
Advisor Prof. Yang Yu

• Brief overview: Designing a kind of bionic sea-urchin (spherical twelve legged) robot for planet unstructured unknown environment exploration tasks.
• Studying and establishing the autonomous motion policy of bionic sea-urchin robot.
• Developed sample efficient, end-to-end reinforcement learning algorithms that allow sea-urchin robots to move over rough terrain and adapt to varying conditions.
• Implemented algorithms using PyTorch and evaluated performance on real world and PyBullet simulated robots.

Research on autonomous adaptation algorithm of multi joint crawling robot to strange terrain

Dalian University of Technology (Department of Engineering Mechanics)
Nov 2019 – May 2020
Research Assistant, Advisor Prof. Haijun Peng
• Brief overview: Design a class of deformable link closed-loop robot, and use genetic algorithm to obtain the optimal motion controller for unfamiliar and complex terrain.
• Design the robot structure and use the Lagrangian equations of the first kind to derive a dynamic model considering the contact force.
• Based on the NEAT algorithm (Evolving Neural Networks through Augmenting Topologies) control scheme, a robot autonomous gait training framework under unstructured terrain is designed.
• Implement the algorithm using NEAT architecture and evaluate the performance on a Pymunk simulated robot.
